# Shape-Language Guide

## Environment kit

![Environment shape-language board](style-frames/environment-shape-language.jpg)

### Trees

The first authored kit uses 8–10 excellent forms, not hundreds of unique assets:

- three ancient conifers at 22–32 m with different trunk taper, base flare, crown break, and dead branch rhythm;
- two wind-shaped coastal pines at 10–18 m with strong lean and horizontal crown masses;
- three broadleaf forms at 12–24 m with columnar, round-spreading, and asymmetric leaning crowns;
- optional snag and sapling variants derived from the same materials.

Each family needs LOD0/LOD1/LOD2 plus a reviewed distant mass/impostor. Scale variation stays within believable age bands. Mirror, yaw, lean, crown tint, dead-branch mask, and ground cluster provide instance variety without destroying species recognition.

Reject perfect conical pines, spherical crowns, identical root flares, trunkless horizon blobs, or thin distant alpha noise.

### Rocks and shell geology

- Six base forms cover 0.4–1 m path stones, 1–2.5 m cluster rocks, and 3–5 m anchor boulders.
- Shapes use erosion shelves, plate breaks, undercuts, and gravity; they are never uniformly scaled icosahedra.
- Shell influence appears in stratified plates and restrained cyan/mineral seams, not in literal turtle-scale patterns on every stone.
- Cluster layouts use one dominant, one support, and small grounding pieces with partial burial and vegetation overlap.

### Props

Props use practical coastal craft: timber, rope/lashing, woven fibre, shell-stone, dark metal, ceramic, cloth, and rare cyan inlay.

- Benches: 1.6–2 m long, 0.45 m seat height, visibly load-bearing.
- Signs: 1.4–2 m total height, asymmetric crosspiece, no floating placard.
- Lanterns: 0.35–0.55 m bodies with framed glow and mounting logic.
- Planters/baskets/crates: grouped by a visible gardening, trade, maintenance, or domestic story.
- Carts/firewood/tools/drainage: placed at plausible work zones, not scattered as filler.
- Wind chimes/ribbons: secondary motion accents, sparse enough to remain special.

### Architecture trim

- Openings have a shell-stone or timber surround, sill, drip edge, and visible reveal.
- Roof edges have fascia thickness, runoff logic, and a readable silhouette.
- Thresholds bridge material changes and protect interaction clearance.
- Rails use thick posts, functional connections, and player-safe spacing.
- Plinths ground buildings into shell geology with moss/runoff/contact variation.
- Cyan inlay marks selected civic, ancient, or turtle-connected structures only.

## Turtle and wildlife

![Turtle and wildlife shape-language board](style-frames/wildlife-turtle-shape-language.jpg)

### World-turtle

- Head silhouette is broad and architectural, with a heavy brow and hooked beak breaking the front curve.
- Visible eye diameter targets roughly 4–6% of head height; the brow and socket dominate, not the eyeball.
- Beak projects roughly 18–25% of head length and shows keratin growth/chip rhythm.
- Neck exposes at least 1.5 head lengths in selected mid/distant compositions and uses large folded bands before small wrinkles.
- Front flipper reads as a tapered hydrofoil several head lengths long, with strong leading edge and softer trailing damage.
- Major scars, barnacle colonies, algae, and cyan seams are asymmetric landmarks that remain stable across LODs.
- No smile curve, round muzzle, paired frontal catchlights, eyebrow expression, or centered head-on acceptance framing.

### Crownwood songbird

- Compact body, believable beak/feet, slightly crested or broken crown silhouette.
- Perch, hop, takeoff, flap, glide, and land poses must remain readable below 1 m screen scale.
- Moss/ochre accents stay on crown, wing edge, or chest—not as full fantasy plumage.

### Shell hare

- Long-legged, athletic hare anatomy rather than round rabbit mascot proportions.
- Ear angle, spine curve, and hind-leg silhouette distinguish rest, forage, alert, hop, and gentle flee.
- Moss marking is a muted dorsal accent, not literal vegetation geometry on every agent.

### Gentle grazer

- Small deer-like proportions with narrow legs, sloped back, light head crest/ear silhouette, and cautious calm stance.
- Antler/horn language stays minimal and non-threatening.
- The grazer is a rare middle-distance habitat event, not a constant village pet.

### Insect groups

- Butterflies read through two or three wing-shape/color families.
- Dragonflies use long horizontal motion and water-edge schedules.
- Fireflies are represented by bounded dark bodies plus restrained warm/cyan points; glow never becomes a particle fog.

### Galecrest and ocean groups

- Seabirds favor long wing/glide silhouettes; close flapping is range-limited.
- Rays use calm banking and surface shadow; dolphins use surface/arc/dive cycles.
- Ocean animals are scale references and rare discoveries, not synchronized spectacle.

## Animation rule

Shape recognition must survive motion. Primary locomotion uses strong whole-body arcs; secondary feathers, ears, foliage, algae, cloth, and spray follow with reduced amplitude. Reduced Motion keeps slow pose changes and removes exaggerated flutter or rapid response.
